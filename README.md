What Is PRP Therapy?
Platelet-Rich Plasma (PRP) therapy is a regenerative treatment that uses concentrated platelets extracted from your own blood. Platelets are rich in growth factors that help repair tissues, stimulate cell regeneration, and improve circulation.
The procedure begins with a small blood draw. The blood is then processed in a centrifuge to separate platelet-rich plasma from other blood components. The concentrated plasma is carefully injected into targeted areas of the scalp where hair thinning is present.
Because PRP uses your own blood, the treatment is considered minimally invasive and has a low risk of allergic reactions.
Why Hair Loss Occurs
Hair follicles naturally cycle through phases of growth, rest, and shedding. Various factors can interrupt this cycle, leading to noticeable thinning over time.
Some of the most common causes include:
Hereditary pattern baldness
Hormonal fluctuations
Nutritional deficiencies
Stress and illness
Aging
Certain medications
Autoimmune conditions
Seeking treatment during the early stages of hair thinning often improves the likelihood of preserving active follicles.
How PRP Supports Hair Growth
Platelets contain naturally occurring proteins known as growth factors. These biological messengers help stimulate cellular repair and encourage healthier tissue function.
When injected into the scalp, PRP may help:
Increase blood supply around hair follicles
Stimulate inactive follicles
Improve follicle strength
Extend the active growth phase of hair
Reduce excessive hair shedding
Although individual responses vary, many patients notice gradual improvements in hair texture and density over several months.
Scientific Evidence Behind PRP
PRP has become one of the most extensively studied regenerative treatments for androgenetic alopecia.
A 2022 systematic review involving multiple clinical studies concluded that PRP significantly improved hair density and overall hair count in many patients compared with baseline measurements.
Research published in the Journal of Cosmetic Dermatology reported measurable increases in hair thickness and reductions in hair shedding after a structured PRP treatment protocol.
According to the American Academy of Dermatology, approximately 80 million Americans experience hereditary hair loss, making it one of the most common cosmetic concerns treated by dermatology professionals.
Clinical evidence continues to grow, with many studies supporting PRP as an effective adjunctive treatment for suitable candidates.
Who Is an Ideal Candidate?
PRP therapy may be recommended for individuals experiencing:
Early-stage male pattern hair loss
Female pattern hair thinning
Diffuse hair shedding
Hair weakening associated with aging
Mild to moderate thinning after stress-related hair loss
Patients with completely inactive follicles or certain medical conditions may require alternative treatment options following a professional consultation.
What Happens During Treatment?
A typical appointment usually lasts less than one hour and includes several simple steps.
Consultation
A provider evaluates the scalp, reviews medical history, and determines whether PRP is appropriate.
Blood Collection
A small blood sample is obtained using a standard blood draw.
Plasma Preparation
The sample is processed to isolate highly concentrated platelet-rich plasma.
Precision Injections
The plasma is injected into targeted areas of the scalp using fine needles.
Most patients return to normal activities shortly after treatment with minimal downtime.

Results and Expectations
Hair restoration is a gradual biological process rather than an overnight transformation.
Many providers recommend an initial series of treatments followed by maintenance sessions to support continued follicle activity. Patients often report reduced shedding first, followed by gradual improvements in hair thickness, density, and overall appearance.
Results depend on factors including age, genetics, lifestyle, overall health, and the severity of hair loss.

Frequently Asked Questions
Is PRP painful?
Most patients experience only mild discomfort, and topical numbing cream is commonly used to improve comfort.
How many sessions are usually recommended?
Treatment plans vary, but many providers suggest multiple initial sessions followed by maintenance appointments based on individual progress.
Are there side effects?
Temporary redness, mild swelling, or scalp tenderness may occur but generally resolve within a few days.
How soon can improvements be noticed?
Many individuals begin observing visible improvements within three to six months as healthier hair growth cycles develop.
